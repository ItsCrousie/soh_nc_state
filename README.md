# NC State Scroll of Honor

> *Honoring NC State University alumni and students who gave their lives in service to our country — from the trenches of World War I to the modern battlefield.*

---

## Overview

The **Scroll of Honor** is a searchable, client-side web memorial built in collaboration with NC State University's **Jeffrey Wright Military & Veterans Services** office. It preserves the stories of over 430 NC State alumni and students who made the ultimate sacrifice across more than seven decades of American military conflict.

Each record was individually researched and compiled from military archives, yearbooks, family records, MACR (Missing Air Crew Reports), FindAGrave, and the ABMC (American Battle Monuments Commission). Many entries contain detailed, first-hand accounts of the circumstances of each service member's death — stories that might otherwise be lost.

---

## Features

- **Instant search** — filter by name, conflict, service branch, or class year with no page reloads
- **432 hand-researched records** spanning WWI through the Gulf War and 9/11
- **Rich detail modals** — rank, unit, cause of death, hometown, and extended historical notes for each individual
- **Nomination form integration** — allows the public to submit new service members for inclusion
- **Fully client-side** — zero backend, zero dependencies, no build step; runs from a single HTML file
- **Responsive design** — mobile-first layout built for any screen size
- **Accessible pagination** — keyboard navigable, with Escape-to-close modal support

---

## Technical Stack

| Layer | Technology |
|---|---|
| Structure | Semantic HTML5 |
| Styling | Vanilla CSS with custom properties (no framework) |
| Logic | Vanilla JavaScript (ES6+) |
| Fonts | Google Fonts — Playfair Display, Source Serif 4, DM Sans |
| Data | Inline JSON (compiled from primary source research) |
| Hosting | GitHub Pages / static file host |

No npm. No bundler. No framework. The entire application ships as a single `.html` file — by design — to ensure it remains accessible and maintainable by non-developers working at Jeffery Wright Military and Veteran Services at NC State.

## Data & Research

Each record in the registry includes some combination of:

- Full name, rank, and service branch
- Class year and area of study
- Hometown
- Date and location of death
- Unit / assignment
- Cause of death (KIA, DOW, MIA/DD, DNB, etc.)
- Extended notes drawn from primary and archival sources

Records were sourced from:

- NC State University yearbooks (1914–1950s)
- U.S. National Archives (MACR, enlistment records)
- American Battle Monuments Commission (ABMC)
- Find A Grave / Fold3
- Family histories and obituaries
- Published unit histories and personal memoirs

Notable entries include **Maj. Oliver Horton** (featured in HBO's *Band of Brothers*), **Col. George "Sinbad" Holloman** (for whom Holloman Air Force Base, NM is named), and **Ensign Roger Allen** (one of the crew lost in the Flight 19 rescue mission — the inspiration for the Bermuda Triangle legend).

## Nominating a Service Member

If you know of an NC State alumnus or student who served and gave their life for our country, use the nomination link in the application header or contact the **Jeffrey Wright Military & Veterans Services** office directly.

Records are reviewed and added manually to preserve the accuracy and integrity of the registry.
